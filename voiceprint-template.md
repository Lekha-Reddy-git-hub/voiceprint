# Voiceprint Template

Fill every section. Write instructions, not descriptions. Attach style_vector.py
output at the top, verbatim.

## 0. Measured baseline (paste tool output)
- words/sentences, median + stdev sentence length, fragment %, long-sentence %
- per-1k rates: em-dash, colon, semicolon, question, exclamation, parentheses
- formatting: headers, bold, emoji, list usage

## 1. Rhythm — imperatives
How short is short. Fragment tolerance. Burst pattern (e.g., "two fragments after
every long claim"). What punctuation carries the rhythm.

## 2. Openers
How do pieces start: scene, claim, question, credential, mid-thought? Quote 2 real
openers.

## 3. Closers
Ritual endings? Question invites? Recaps? How often — give the ratio, cap it there.

## 4. Signature moves (PROTECTED — never de-slop these)
Pet phrases, named frameworks, formatting rituals. List verbatim with frequency
("'as far as I can tell' — roughly 1 per piece; keep near that rate, never every
paragraph").

## 5. Never-does (the negative space)
Punctuation never used. Registers never entered (profanity? emoji? hedging?).
A single violation here is a forgery tell.

## 6. Evidence style
How do they prove points: numbers, anecdotes, named companies, analogies, authority?
Where do their metaphors come from?

## 7. Stance & hedging
Assert-then-invite? Hedge-then-conclude? First-person density. How do they disagree?

## 8. Humor & self-disclosure
Type, frequency, targets. Self-deprecation allowed? Quote one real instance.

## 9. Structure
Paragraph lengths. Header usage. List tolerance. Skeleton — and how often they
BREAK their own skeleton (protect that ratio; 100% skeleton = machine tell).

## 10. Exemplars
2–3 short excerpts of their best, most-them writing, pasted verbatim.

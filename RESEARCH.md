# Research this design stands on

**Voices are measurable (stylometry / authorship attribution).**
Stamatatos, *A Survey of Modern Authorship Attribution Methods*, JASIST 2009.
Function words are used largely unconsciously and are topic-independent;
character n-grams, punctuation and sentence-length distributions identify authors.
→ Why Organ 1 measures numbers before asking for impressions.

**AI assistance homogenizes writing.**
Padmakumar & He, *Does Writing with Language Models Reduce Content Diversity?*,
ICLR 2024 (arXiv:2309.05196). Writing with an LLM measurably increases similarity
between different authors' text. → The problem voiceprint exists to reverse.

**AI has statistical tells, best measured as excess frequency.**
Kobak et al., *Delving into LLM-assisted writing in biomedical publications through
excess vocabulary*, Science Advances 2025 (arXiv:2406.07016). "Delves" rose 28-fold;
style words dominate the excess. → Why the Audit counts frequencies vs a baseline
instead of flagging single words.

**Personalization works by showing the model your own writing.**
Salemi et al., *LaMP: When Large Language Models Meet Personalization*, ACL 2024.
Retrieving the user's own items into context measurably improves personalized
generation. → Why profiles embed verbatim exemplars.

**Feature-based scoring with human override has 25+ years of operational history.**
ETS e-rater (Attali & Burstein 2006; ETS research reports) has scored GRE/TOEFL
essays on measurable features alongside human raters since 1999. → The Audit
organ's design: feature scores + human judgment, never machine verdicts alone.

**Verdict-style AI detection is unreliable and biased.**
Liang, Yuksekgonul, Mao, Wu, Zou, *GPT detectors are biased against non-native
English writers*, Patterns 2023 (arXiv:2304.02819). Over half of non-native TOEFL
essays were falsely flagged as AI. → Why the Audit outputs counted evidence and
assumptions, never "AI: yes/no".

**Cautionary tale: universal rubrics create monoculture.**
Test-prep template essays optimized the AES rubric; LLM prose optimized average
preference. Both converged on beige. → Why every voiceprint score is
baseline-relative: you vs you, never you vs "ideal writing".
